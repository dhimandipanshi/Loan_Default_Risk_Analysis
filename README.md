# 🏦 Loan Default Risk Analysis at Horizon Financial Group
### Identifying key drivers of loan defaults to strengthen underwriting decisions and reduce portfolio risk
---

# 🚀 Executive Summary

Horizon Financial Group observed that approximately **1 in 4 personal loans (25%)** issued during **2024–2025** resulted in default—more than double the organization's target default rate of **12%**.
To identify the primary drivers of loan defaults, I analyzed borrower profiles and loan application data using **SQL, Python (Pandas), and Excel**. The analysis focused on four critical underwriting factors:

- Credit Score
- Debt-to-Income (DTI) Ratio
- Loan Purpose
- Employment Stability

The findings provide evidence-based recommendations to improve Horizon Financial's credit approval process by identifying high-risk borrower segments and proposing practical underwriting thresholds.

### 📈 Recommended Underwriting Policy

- Minimum Credit Score: **650**
- Maximum Debt-to-Income Ratio: **40%**
- Minimum Employment Tenure: **2 Years**
- Enhanced review for higher-risk loan purposes

These recommendations aim to improve portfolio quality while reducing default risk and increasing underwriting consistency.

---

# 🧩 Business Problem

Horizon Financial Group issued more than **600 personal loans** across **2024–2025**, but approximately **25%** of borrowers defaulted—well above the company's target of **12%**.

The Risk Management team required a data-driven analysis to determine:

- Which borrower segments contribute most to loan defaults?
- How do credit score, DTI, loan purpose, and employment influence repayment behaviour?
- What underwriting thresholds should be introduced to reduce future credit losses?

# 📊 Results & Key Insights

## 🔑 Key Findings

### 1. Credit Score
Lower credit score segments consistently exhibited the highest default rates, making credit score the strongest predictor of repayment risk.

### 2. Debt-to-Income Ratio
Borrowers with higher DTI ratios demonstrated significantly greater default risk, indicating that debt burden is a key underwriting metric.

### 3. Loan Purpose
Default rates varied across loan purposes, suggesting that certain loan categories require stricter approval criteria.

### 4. Employment Stability
Borrowers with shorter employment histories were more likely to default, while longer employment tenure was associated with stronger repayment performance.

---

# 💡 Business Recommendations

### 1. Strengthen Credit Score Requirements
- Require a minimum credit score of **650**
- Apply enhanced underwriting for applicants below the threshold

### 2. Introduce DTI Approval Limits
- Approve borrowers with **DTI ≤ 40%**
- Refer higher DTI applications for manual review

### 3. Prioritize Employment Stability
- Prefer borrowers with at least **2 years of continuous employment**
- Apply additional verification for shorter employment histories

### 4. Apply Risk-Based Underwriting
- Introduce additional assessment for higher-risk loan purposes
- Support consistent lending decisions using standardized risk criteria
